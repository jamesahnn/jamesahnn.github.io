Projects

Tugolo

Role

UX Designer

Project type

Team work with designers and developers to integrate a travel platform

Duration

May 2019 - August 2019

Tools

Figma, Illustrator

Problem

How can I connect these travellers with locals and help them experience real and authentic cultures?

Locals all across the globe can and want to provide the exact travel experiences that travelers are looking for. But the travelers and locals can’t find each other in a trusted way, and locals don't have the required tools to bring their trips to life and facilitate the transactions, and the business.

Solution

Introduce travellers to locals

In order for travellers to connect with locals, the landing page had to showcase a variety of them and the possible services that they provide. The point of the landing page was to introduce travellers to locals rather than the experiences and accommodations.

Prototype 1:

Prototype 1 was based off of the original landing page with new features

Problem:

The problem with this prototype was that it was towards showcasing locations of the possible experiences.

Prototype 2:

This prototype was given a new, different look. Icons were added and a new bubble diagram of experiences was designed.

Problem:

The problem with this prototype was it still didn’t satisfy Tugolo’s goal of connecting travellers to locals very well.

Wireframing of Final Prototype

How might we focus the traveller’s attention towards local’s profiles?

For the landing page to match the criteria for the business, it had to be re-designed such that it advertised  the locals so travellers can easily connect with them.

Wireframe 1

First, the landing page needed to provide a quick access to the local’s profile and their offerings. The first wireframe presented the top friends of Tugolo right under the search bar.

User Feedback:

"Saw the top friends section but I found myself scrolling down to see the other features almost immediately"

"It definitely caught my attention but not enough to spend more than a few seconds on it"

User testing on first wireframe

Wireframe 2

The second wireframe had a new addition called the best friends list. This allowed the landing page to not only provide its top friends but also its best friends for the traveller.

User Feedback:

“The best friends feature definitely caught my eye more than the top friends section”

“I found myself curious to see what the best friends section was about because I don’t see websites doing this often”

User testing on second wireframe

Second wireframe proved to grasp more attention towards Tugolo's friends

After user testing the first wireframe, I wasn’t satisfied with the amount of attention the Top Friends section had. As I moved on with the second wireframe, the Best Friends section definitely got user’s attention as 92% of them took 3 or more seconds to check it out.

Mobile Version

The mobile design was compressed from the final webpage. All the necessary components were formatted into cards that were easily viewable by swiping through them.

Registration and Creating Pages

This step was an important part for Tugolo as it was the process of bringing friends and partners into the platform. For usability purposes, I made sure that all the pages were consistent and followed a strict format to further ease the process.

Becoming a partner of Tugolo

Partner Registration Form

Once the partner chooses what category they fall into, it will ask for their personal information. Each page of information will slide up, following a new page of information. As a new page comes up, the horizontal scrolling menu slides accordingly.

Provider/friend send out forms

Used to send out registration forms for the friend's partners and providers.

Create pages

It is very important for partners to upload their offerings accurately. The amount of information needed for them to complete just one listing is substantial. To make it as effortless and simple as possible, all the create pages follow the same order.

Final Deliverables for Offerings

Once Tugolo’s friends finish creating their offerings, they will have their own profile and their listings will become available for the travellers to see.

Takeaways

Having the opportunity to work at a start up with a flexible but fast paced environment really taught me how to manage time better. With a set deadline, it didn’t matter how I got it done and this allowed me to manage work during and outside work hours. Since it was a start up, the work done for me was close to none so my teammates and I had to start from scratch and build our way up from there.

Nothing is ever finished. Learning this allowed me to design in a way that would be easily editable in the future. Altough my designs may be finished, there is always room for improvement and I learned that I had to set the future designer to continue what I started just through the designs.
